The Bonus Lessons are: Lessons 17–20 in Part A and Lessons 37–40 in Part B. These lessons should NOT be taught in class during the SMART course. Steps of the method

  • Class routine

    a. Give a brief explanation of the Educational Objective in English.

    Make sure you clearly explain the objective and the skills students are expected to develop in the lesson. This shouldn't take more than a few seconds.

  • Step 1. Go over the language target.

    1. a. Play the video of the target structure. This is particularly important for students to hear the language in context and become familiar with the natural use of the structure.

    2. b. Clarify any questions students may have about the video.

  • Step 2. Go over the practice activity.

    Explain to your students that they will listen to 3 short texts: a text message, an instagram post and an email. Then they will read the texts meaningfully. Tell students to be aware of pronunciation, intonation, and fluency.

    1. a. Show the first text and play the audio. Ask students to read along.

    2. b. Introduce the new vocabulary and/or expressions.

      - Show the first vocabulary slide found in the text.

      - Select one student and have him/her read the word out loud. If the pronunciation is incorrect, have him/her follow the color chart guide found on the slide. Be sure to give constant feedback on pronunciation, and/or intonation using the Color Chart.

      - Explain the word using perception and production.

      - Continue in the same fashion with the rest of the words.

    3. c. Show the first text and play the audio again as students read along.

      - Select a student and have him/her read the text.

      - Follow the correction process for pronunciation, intonation, and fluency.

    4. d. Repeat steps a to c for the rest of the texts.

  • Step 3. Go over the reading activity.

    Each lesson has a different practice activity, and a different procedure. Check the corresponding lesson to learn the correct procedure for the activity.

Reading

( 🕓 Est. Time: 20 min)


Reading 1. Text Message


Dad! Guess what?

I made the city soccer team!! We have practice every Tuesday and Thursday after work. I'm really excited, but I'm also a little nervous.

Can you come to my first game next weekend?

Vocabulary


made Def. Today I make. Yesterday I made.
Ex. I made a cake yesterday.


Reading 2. Instagram Post


First game next week! 💙

Our new uniforms are ready! I have number 10 this season! Our coach has a plan for our first game, and everyone is ready.

🍀 We can't wait to play!

Vocabulary


uniforms Def. Special clothes that people wear for work, school, or sports.
Ex. The players wear new uniforms for every game.
number Def. A symbol used for counting or to identify something.
Ex. My favorite number is ten.
season Def. A period of the year when people play a sport or do an activity.
Ex. The next soccer season starts in May.


Reading 3. Email


Subject: Important information

Hello Martin,

You have locker number 30 this season.

Your locker has space for your uniform, shoes, and other items. Please keep it clean after every practice. See you on Tuesday!

Coach Ramirez

Vocabulary


information Def. Facts or details that help you know something.
Ex. I need more information about the game.
locker Def. A small space where you keep your personal things at work, school, or the gym.
Ex. My locker number is 30.
items Def. Things or objects.
Ex. Backpacks, notebooks, and pencils are school items.

Practice

( 🕓 Est. Time: 15 min)


Spinning wheel


Procedure:

In this activity, students will use a spinning wheel to practice have and has as they answer comprehension questions related to the texts read in class.


a. Spin the wheel and ask a student to read the question found on the picture where it stops.

b. Have a second student answer the question. Follow the correction process.

c. Continue in the same way with the remaining pictures and different students until all 10 questions have been answered.

Extra activities


If there is any extra time, you may have students engage in further practice related to the contents of the lesson. You can have them perform any of the following activities:

  • Challenge

    This activity is intended for the students to practice the elements of the lesson by themselves in a fun way. However, you may also go over the activity in class and take advantage of the opportunity to provide extra practice.

    Choose a student and have him/her read the first item and the four options. Ask the student to choose the correct answer. Then click on the student’s answer. If it is correct, the platform will move to the next item. If it is wrong, the student can keep trying until the correct answer is chosen.

  • Spelling Bee Contest

    This activity helps students practice spelling words found in the current lesson in a fun way. Choose a word from the dialogues in the lesson and call on a student. Then say the word once and use it in a sentence. Have the student pronounce the word again and spell it as you write it on the whiteboard. If the spelling of the word is right, congratulate the student. If it is wrong, cross out the word and write the correct word on the board. Then ask the student to say the word and spell it again, now following the correct spelling. Use the Color Chart to correct pronunciation.

  • Reading Practice

    This activity is intended for students to continue developing their reading skills. Have students read the short dialogues from the lesson once more. Follow the procedure for the reading activity.

  • Questions and answers related to the language targets.

    This is a controlled activity that leads students to the structures considered in the objective of the lesson, stressing on the areas where students had more trouble.
